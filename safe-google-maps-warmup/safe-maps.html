Safer Google Maps Warm Up

When a user uses Google Maps to get walking directions from one place to the next, Google Maps returns to the user the fastest route(s) that get the user to their destination. However, the app currently does not take into account safety, a factor that can be especially important when walking through giant cities like SF. Let's try and fix that!

In this warmup for next week, your job will be to determine a mechanism for "scoring" each possible path returned by GoogleMaps when we ask it for directions. You can see a path's score by clicking on the route and looking at the number below the map. A higher score means a more dangerous path. Currently, we score every path as 0.
